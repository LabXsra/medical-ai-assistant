Disease: Pneumonia

Overview:
Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs of one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, making breathing difficult.

Symptoms:
- Fever
- Cough
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Chills
- Fatigue
- Rapid breathing

Causes:
Bacteria, viruses, fungi, or aspiration of food and liquids.

Risk Factors:
- Elderly
- Smoking
- Chronic lung disease
- Diabetes
- Weak immunity

Diagnosis:
Chest X-ray, blood tests, sputum culture, pulse oximetry.

Treatment:
Antibiotics, antivirals, oxygen therapy, fluids, and hospitalization if severe.

Prevention:
Vaccination, smoking cessation, hand hygiene.

Emergency Signs:
Severe breathing difficulty, low oxygen levels, confusion, chest pain.
