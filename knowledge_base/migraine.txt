Disease: Migraine

Overview:
Migraine is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent moderate to severe headaches.

Symptoms:
- Severe headache
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Light sensitivity
- Sound sensitivity
- Blurred vision

Causes:
Genetics, hormonal changes, stress, lack of sleep, certain foods.

Risk Factors:
- Female gender
- Family history
- Stress
- Sleep deprivation

Diagnosis:
Medical history, neurological examination, MRI or CT scan if required.

Treatment:
Pain relievers, triptans, preventive medications, adequate hydration and sleep.

Prevention:
Regular sleep, stress management, avoiding dietary triggers.

Emergency Signs:
Sudden severe headache, confusion, weakness, vision loss.
