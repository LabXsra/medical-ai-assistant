Disease: Hypertension

Overview:
Hypertension is a chronic condition where blood pressure remains consistently elevated, increasing the risk of cardiovascular disease.

Symptoms:
- Often no symptoms
- Headache
- Blurred vision
- Dizziness
- Chest pain

Causes:
Genetics, obesity, excessive salt intake, kidney disease, stress.

Risk Factors:
- Age
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Family history

Diagnosis:
Repeated blood pressure measurements and laboratory evaluation.

Treatment:
Lifestyle modification, antihypertensive medications, weight management.

Prevention:
Healthy diet, regular exercise, reduced salt intake, avoiding smoking.

Emergency Signs:
Blood pressure above 180/120 mmHg with chest pain, vision changes, or confusion.
