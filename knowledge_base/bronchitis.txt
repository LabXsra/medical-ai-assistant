Disease: Bronchitis

Overview:
Bronchitis is inflammation of the bronchial tubes that carry air to the lungs. It may be acute or chronic.

Symptoms:
- Persistent cough
- Mucus production
- Wheezing
- Chest discomfort
- Fatigue
- Mild fever

Causes:
Viruses, bacteria, smoking, air pollution.

Risk Factors:
- Smoking
- Air pollution
- Weak immune system
- Chronic lung disease

Diagnosis:
Clinical examination, chest X-ray if pneumonia is suspected.

Treatment:
Rest, hydration, bronchodilators, cough medicine, antibiotics if bacterial.

Prevention:
Avoid smoking, wash hands regularly, influenza vaccination.

Emergency Signs:
Difficulty breathing, coughing blood, high fever.
